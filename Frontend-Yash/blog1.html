Nutrients: The Building Blocks of Life

BY : Yuvraj Yadav
Nutrients are essential substances that our bodies need to grow, repair, and function properly. They provide us with energy, build and maintain tissues, and support various bodily processes.

There are six major classes of nutrients:

1) Carbohydrates : These provide the primary source of energy for our bodies. They are found in grains, fruits, vegetables, and dairy products.

2) Proteins: Essential for building and repairing tissues, proteins are found in meat, poultry, fish, eggs, beans, and nuts.

3) Fats: While often associated with unhealthy eating, healthy fats are essential for energy, hormone production, and cell function. They are found in olive oil, avocados, nuts, and seeds.

4) Vitamins: These are organic compounds that our bodies need in small amounts. They help regulate various bodily functions. Examples include Vitamin C, Vitamin D, and Vitamin B12.

5) Minerals : Inorganic substances that our bodies need in small amounts, minerals help with bone health, nerve function, and fluid balance. Examples include calcium, iron, and potassium.

6) Water: While not technically a nutrient, water is essential for all bodily functions. It helps regulate temperature, transport nutrients, and eliminate waste.
